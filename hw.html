[ time change Brownian motion ] Last week in class we defined that the (marginal) distribution of the Brownian motion is $B_t ∼\mathcal{N}(0, t)$ and its moment generating function.
Given a Gamma process (see Wiki definition) $\Gamma(t;\gamma,\lambda)$, we now consider a time change Brownian motion $W_{\Gamma(t;\gamma,\lambda)}$. The random time o’clock $\Gamma(t;\gamma,\lambda)$ is used to model rapid or slow market activity during a trading day. What is the moment generating function of $W_{\Gamma(t;\gamma,\lambda)}$?
That is, what is $E[e^{\theta W_{\Gamma(t;\gamma,\lambda)}}]$? (Hint: conditioning on the gamma process is useful.)
